Montréal at a Glance
Island city on the St. Lawrence River
Montréal is built on an island at the confluence of the St. Lawrence and Ottawa Rivers. The city's name comes from the three-peaked hill Mont Royal that anchors it. With over 2 million residents, it's Canada's second-largest city and the world's largest French-speaking city outside Paris. Known for its underground city (RÉSO), legendary bagels, poutine, jazz festivals, and joie de vivre.

Money & Payments
Canadian Dollars (CAD)
Currency is CAD. Most places accept Visa/Mastercard. American Express accepted widely downtown. US Dollars sometimes accepted near tourist areas but at a poor rate — use ATMs for best exchange. Tipping: 15–20% at restaurants, 10–15% for taxis. HST (sales tax) is 15% in Québec (5% federal + 9.975% provincial).

Société de transport de Montréal

The STM Métro

4 lines · 68 stations · Runs daily. Clean, safe, and efficient — the backbone of getting around Montréal.

🎫
Fares (2024)
Single fare: $3.75 CAD. 10-trip: $32.50. Monthly OPUS card: ~$101. Tap your OPUS card or buy tickets at any station vending machine.
Metro Lines
1
Green Line — Ligne Verte
Angrignon ↔ Honoré-Beaugrand · 27 stations
The longest line, running east-west through the heart of the city. Key tourist stops: Lionel-Groulx (transfer), Guy-Concordia (Crescent St nightlife), Peel (downtown shopping), McGill (downtown core), Berri-UQAM (central hub), Papineau (Gay Village), Pie-IX (Olympic Stadium).
2
Orange Line — Ligne Orange
Côte-Vertu ↔ Montmorency · 31 stations
U-shaped line through downtown and east. Key stops: Bonaventure (Central Station, Palais des congrès), Square-Victoria–OACI (Old Montréal access), Place-d'Armes (Old Montréal, Basilica), Sherbrooke (Plateau), Mont-Royal (Plateau main street), Rosemont, Jean-Talon (Little Italy, Jean-Talon Market).
5
Blue Line — Ligne Bleue
Snowdon ↔ Saint-Michel · 12 stations
East-west through midtown. Key stops: Snowdon (transfer to Orange), Côte-des-Neiges (student area), Université-de-Montréal, Édouard-Montpetit, Outremont (trendy cafés), De Castelnau (Mile End area).
4
Yellow Line — Ligne Jaune
Berri-UQAM ↔ Longueuil · 3 stations
Short line crossing the St. Lawrence River to the South Shore. Key stops: Berri-UQAM (major hub — connects all 3 other lines), Jean-Drapeau (Île Sainte-Hélène, La Ronde, Casino, Biosphere), Longueuil–Université-de-Sherbrooke (south shore).
Tourist-Friendly Stations
Hours & Tips
Weekdays: ~5:30 AM – 1:00 AM
Weekends: ~5:30 AM – 1:30 AM
Buses: Continue overnight ("bus de nuit") on main routes

Tips: Stations are underground and covered — great shelter in rain or -40° winter. OPUS cards can be loaded at all stations. Free transfers within 120 minutes of first tap. Bikes allowed on trains on weekends (off-peak).
